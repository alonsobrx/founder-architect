The June 16 Vote — What Cameron County Must Decide

Arnoldo Alonso · Brasidas Strategies

On June 3, 2026, the Cameron County Commissioners Court tabled a vote on one of the largest tax abatement requests in the history of the Rio Grande Valley. The vote is now scheduled for June 16, 2026.

The company requesting the abatement is Saronic Technologies. The proposal is called Port Alpha — a proposed autonomous shipyard on 835 acres at the Port of Brownsville. The tax abatement requested would run 19 years and is valued at approximately $852,862,500.

The company has raised $2.525 billion in venture capital. It is currently valued at $7.5 billion. It has 271 verified employees. It is asking Cameron County — one of the poorest counties in the United States — to forgo nearly a billion dollars in tax revenue over the next two decades, in exchange for promises.

Before June 16, every commissioner should be able to answer the following:

  • What is the legally binding minimum job count? What is the clawback if it is not met?
  • What is the full wage distribution — not averages, full distribution?
  • What is the independent cost-benefit analysis, not prepared by the company?
  • What happens to the abatement if the company is acquired, goes public, or relocates?
  • Who bears the infrastructure cost — roads, water, power, port expansion?

These are not hostile questions. They are the minimum due diligence any responsible elected official owes the people who elected them.
