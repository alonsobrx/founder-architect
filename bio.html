Biography

Arnoldo Alonso is a combat-decorated United States Army veteran who served thirteen years as a Staff Sergeant. He is service-connected disabled, lifelong resident of the Rio Grande Valley, and the founder of Brasidas Strategies — an independent research and technology firm operating at the intersection of public policy, data science, and neurointelligent systems architecture.

He is the principal architect of Ayala™ — a proprietary neurointelligence research platform developed over more than a decade. Ayala™ is designed to serve veterans, the visually impaired, the disabled, and their families — equipping them with the same analytical tools used by institutional investors and policy professionals.

His public interest research applies those tools to the critical decisions that shape communities. Every claim is sourced. Every calculation is shown. Every publication is free and open to the public.

Brasidas Strategies has no financial interest in any subject it analyzes, no client relationships with any party to any transaction it covers, and no affiliation with any lobbying organization or government agency. Independence is not a policy — it is the foundation.

Mission

The goal is mass education. To give ordinary people — workers, families, veterans, neighbors — the same analytical power used by those who make decisions that affect their lives. Knowledge is the only antidote to extraction.

Every publication produced by Brasidas Strategies is free. Every calculation is open. Every source is cited. The work is offered to the public record without restriction — reproduce it, share it, use it at hearings, use it to question your representatives.
